Awarding the best works in technology, handbuilt by students.

Quiver wants to recognize the talent and hardwork behind Machine Projects (MPs) that go beyond expectations. Quiver is a platform to showcase the projects that stands out from the rest of the pack made by students who gave more than 100% in order to create a compelling work.

By recognizing their extra effort, students are enticed to go beyond than just simply passing the class in order to create something that they would be proud of.

Most machine projects reach their End-of-Life (EOL) once the term ends but what if those projects can be repurposed in a way to help the students who developed it?

Students spends hundreds of hours developing and tuning their ideas just to create a application/system that was meant to be forgotten once the term ends. Process

All projects submitted or forwarded to the Quiver team will undergo evaluation.

1. Submissions

A.) Endorsed Submissions
A faculty may endorse an MP or a Thesis to be featured on Quiver.
B.) Student Submissions (currently unavailable)
Submissions will undergo initial screenings to identify which projects are student-defined, if there is an existing identical project during the time it was developed, and so on.

2. Evaluation

A panel of judges who are experts or knowledgeable in that research field or speciality will be assigned to their respective category. They will evaluate submissions that have undergone initial screening through a standard rubric assigned for each category available.

Minimum
Requirements

All submissions should first be compliant in all of the listed minimum requirements. The submitting party should be responsible enough to disclose information regarding the project being submitted.

Student-Defined Projects

Student-Defined projects are project types that would require the student to think creatively and innovatively on how to tackle a problem and propose that solution. These types of projects are similar to Web Development Classes, Mobile Development Classes, Startup Classes, Robotics Classes, etc. Side-projects or projects done during their own persona free time is considered a student-defined project.

Student-Made Only Projects

Teams/Individuals who submit their project to Quiver must be a student at that time. Students who have already graduated and have submitted a project to quiver is still considered acceptable as long as they have finished the project while enrolled as a student in DLSU. Quiver's submission rules are retroactive.

Non-Comissioned Projects

Projects should not be a paid project such as freelance projects or internship work. This is done to avoid legal issues with companies or clients that do not want a commissioned worked to be posted online.

Available Categories

As of now, there are only 4 categories that are being considered by the Quiver Team.

- Web Applications (WEBAPDE/WEBDEVE/GAMEDEV)
- Mobile Applications (MOBIDEV/MOBICOM/GAMEDEV)
- Desktop Applications (OBJECTP/GAMEDEV)
- Robotics and Hardware Applications (ROBOTICS)

More categories will be added once the Quiver Team has identified specific classes with student-defined projects.

Side/Individual projects can be considered as one of the 4 categories from above.
